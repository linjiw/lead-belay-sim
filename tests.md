# Manual validation checklist

## Basic sanity
- [ ] Theoretical fall factor increases when fall distance increases at fixed rope-out.
- [ ] Actual fall factor is >= theoretical fall factor when friction participation < 1.
- [ ] Increasing rope dynamic elongation lowers peak force and increases displacement.
- [ ] Increasing quickdraw friction raises peak climber force for otherwise equal settings.
- [ ] Free / lighter belayer reduces peak force and increases belayer lift.
- [ ] Hard tether raises anchor load and reduces belayer displacement.
- [ ] Disabling first clip increases ground-fall risk in low-clip presets.
- [ ] Soft catch timing near rope-loading moment lowers peak force compared to no soft catch.

## Preset review
- [ ] Gym low clip risk: possible low-point near floor with low friction and free belayer.
- [ ] Soft catch with lighter belayer: visibly softer peak and more lift.
- [ ] Hard catch heavy anchored belayer: visibly stiffer catch and higher peak.
- [ ] Multi-pitch near-belay: higher factor, tether-limited belayer motion.

## Mobile UI
- [ ] iPhone width (~390 px) usable without horizontal page scroll.
- [ ] Canvas scales to width.
- [ ] Sliders remain usable with touch.
- [ ] Summary cards wrap cleanly.
